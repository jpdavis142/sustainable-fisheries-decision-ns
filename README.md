# Sustainable Fisheries vs. Economic Growth

## Decision Statement
Should a Nova Scotia fisheries policy advisor implement stricter catch limits to protect marine ecosystems, or maintain current quotas to support short-term economic stability?

## Executive Summary
Marine ecosystems are essential to both environmental sustainability and economic activity, particularly in coastal regions such as Nova Scotia. The fisheries sector plays a central role in the provincial economy, supporting employment, exports, and the long-term viability of many coastal communities. However, increasing pressures from overfishing, climate change, and habitat degradation have raised concerns about the sustainability of marine resources. As fish stocks decline or become more volatile, policymakers face growing urgency to ensure that current practices do not compromise future ecosystem health or economic stability.

The core decision in this project centers on whether stricter catch limits should be implemented to protect marine biodiversity and support long-term sustainability, or whether current quotas should be maintained to preserve short-term economic performance. This decision is complex because it involves a fundamental tradeoff between immediate economic benefits and long-term environmental outcomes. Stricter regulations may allow fish populations to recover and improve ecosystem resilience, but they can also reduce short-term income for fishers and disrupt local economies. Conversely, maintaining current quotas supports near-term economic stability but risks accelerating resource depletion and undermining the future of the industry.

This project adopts a decision-focused and systems-thinking approach to evaluate these competing priorities. By examining the relationships between fishing intensity, fish population dynamics, regulatory intervention, and economic outcomes, the analysis will highlight key feedback loops that influence long-term system behavior. The goal is to provide a structured understanding of how different policy choices align with both economic objectives and sustainability commitments, including those outlined in Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water. Ultimately, this work aims to support more informed and balanced decision-making in fisheries management.
